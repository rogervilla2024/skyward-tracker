Game Overview

Skyward is a crash game - a type of casino game where a multiplier increases from 1x and can "crash" at any random moment. The objective is simple: cash out before the crash happens.

The Core Concept

In Skyward, you'll see a balloon that rises into the sky. As it rises, a multiplier increases. The higher it goes, the bigger your potential win. But at any moment, the balloon can pop (crash), ending the round. If you haven't cashed out by then, you lose your bet.

Step-by-Step Guide to Playing Skyward

Step 1: Place Your Bet

What to do: Before each round, there's a betting window (usually a few seconds). During this time:

  1. Choose your bet amount using the + and - buttons or by typing directly
  2. Click the "Bet" or "Place Bet" button to confirm
  3. Wait for the round to begin

Tip: Start with small bets while you're learning the game mechanics.

Step 2: Watch the Balloon Rise

What happens: Once the round starts:

  1. The colorful balloon begins ascending into the sky
  2. The multiplier starts at 1.00x and increases as the balloon rises
  3. The higher the balloon goes, the faster the multiplier increases
  4. Other players' bets and cash-outs may be visible on screen

Step 3: Decide When to Cash Out

The crucial decision: This is where the game gets exciting. You must decide:

  • Cash out early for a smaller, safer win
  • Hold longer for a potentially bigger multiplier
  • Risk losing everything if the balloon pops

Click the "Cash Out" button at any time to secure your win.

Step 4: Collect or Lose

Two possible outcomes:

  • If you cash out in time: Your bet is multiplied by the current multiplier. Bet $10 and cash out at 3x = $30 return ($20 profit)
  • If the balloon pops before you cash out: You lose your entire bet for that round

Step 5: Repeat

After each round, a new betting window opens, and you can choose to bet again or sit out.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Believing in Patterns

The mistake: "The last 5 rounds crashed early, so the next one will go high."

The reality: Each round is independent. Past results don't affect future outcomes.

Mistake 2: Chasing Losses

The mistake: Increasing bets after losses to recover money.

The reality: This often accelerates losses and can be financially devastating.

Mistake 3: Playing Without Limits

The mistake: Not setting budget or time limits.

The reality: Without limits, it's easy to lose more than intended.

Mistake 4: Waiting Too Long to Cash Out

The mistake: Always holding for big multipliers.
